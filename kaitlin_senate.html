Our Philosophy:

Government spending is the most direct window a citizen has into the government's actual priorities

Data on Government Spending Should Be:

  • Timely
  • Complete
  • Comprehensive
  • Descriptive
  • Interoperable

Timely is ...

  • Ongoing trends can be detected, analyzed
  • Detection of fraud is actionable
  • Predictable
  • Newsworthy
Timely data allows for trends in the data to be detected in real time. For a business analytics firm, this might mean a custom market analysis for their customers. For NGOS and other governmental service providers, they might adjust their resources to better complement federal action. Or for journalists it could mean there's something to report on that's relevant to the public.

Complete Data...

  • is present!
  • And if not, documents why not

Comprehensive

Spending data should include everything feasible and the default mode of spending data should be disclosure. Citizens deserve the true window into federal spending without countless exceptions.

Descriptive

  • Category codes are helpful, but not a replacement for a detailed description of spending
  • Context is important!
  • Metadata helps with context, in an automated way

Interoperable

  • Data can be denormalized, but must be interoperable
  • e.g. Entity registries, category code sets, geographic locations
  • This is where standardization helps
